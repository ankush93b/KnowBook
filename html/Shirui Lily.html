Shirui Lily

Shirui Lily is a rare and endangered pinkish-white flower only found in the Shirui Hills in Ukhrul district of Imphal.

The flower was discovered by British botanist Dr. Frank Kingdon Ward in 1946. The scientific name of flower Lilium Mackliniae was named after his wife Jean Macklin. The Shirui Lily gained international recognition when the Royal Horticultural Society awarded it a prestigious merit at its London Flower Show in 1950.

To commemorate the discovery of flower, Department of Tourism, Imphal organises a festival called Shirui Lily Festival in Ukhrul district.

Blossoms Season

These lilies flowering season spans from April to June, when it grows about a foot tall.
